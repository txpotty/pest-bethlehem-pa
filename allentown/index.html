Common Pest Problems in Allentown, PA and How We Address Them

Allentown, Pennsylvania, with its unique climate and urban and suburban mix, is susceptible to a variety of common pest infestations. Understanding these common problems helps us tailor our pest control services to the specific needs of residents and businesses in the area. At Bethlehem Pest Pros, we have extensive experience dealing with the pests that are most prevalent in the Lehigh Valley, providing targeted and effective solutions for each.

Ant Infestations

Ants are a frequent and often frustrating problem for property owners in Allentown. They are attracted to food sources and moisture and can quickly establish large colonies both indoors and outdoors. Different species of ants require different control methods. For example, carpenter ants can cause structural damage by nesting in wood, while pavement ants are more of a nuisance. We identify the specific ant species, locate their nests, and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the colony and prevent future entry. This may involve baiting, barrier treatments, and identifying and sealing entry points. Our ant control services are comprehensive and designed for long-term relief.

Rodent Problems (Mice and Rats)

Mice and rats are not only unsettling to encounter but can also cause significant damage to property by gnawing on wires, insulation, and structures. They also pose serious health risks by spreading diseases and contaminating food surfaces with their droppings and urine. Rodent infestations can be difficult to eliminate without professional help, as they are prolific breeders and adept at hiding. Our rodent control services in Allentown, PA involve a multi-faceted approach, including inspection, trapping, baiting, exclusion to seal entry points, and sanitation recommendations to remove attractants. We focus on safe and humane removal whenever possible and implement preventative measures to keep them from returning. Learn more on our rodent control page.

Termite Damage

Termites are a silent threat that can cause extensive and costly structural damage to homes and businesses in Allentown before you even realize they are present. These wood-destroying pests feed on cellulose and can compromise the integrity of your property's foundation and framing. Early detection and treatment are crucial to minimize damage. Our termite treatment services include thorough inspections to detect the presence of termites, even in hard-to-reach areas, and the implementation of effective treatment plans to eliminate colonies and protect your property. We use advanced techniques and products to create protective barriers and eradicate active infestations, offering peace of mind against these destructive pests.

Bed Bug Infestations

Bed bugs are notorious for being difficult to get rid of and can cause sleepless nights and itchy bites. These tiny pests hide in cracks and crevices, furniture, and especially mattresses and box springs, coming out at night to feed on blood. Bed bug infestations can spread rapidly and are often brought into homes unknowingly. Our bed bug extermination services in Allentown are comprehensive and involve a combination of inspection, treatment, and follow-up protocols. We utilize a variety of methods, which may include heat treatments, chemical applications, and thorough vacuuming, to effectively eliminate bed bugs at all life stages. We also provide guidance on preventing future infestations.

Cockroach Problems

Cockroaches are not only unsightly but also pose significant health risks, carrying bacteria and allergens that can exacerbate respiratory issues. They are resilient pests that can thrive in various environments and are known for being difficult to eradicate without professional help. Our cockroach control services involve identifying the species of cockroach and implementing targeted treatments that may include baiting, insecticide applications, and sanitation recommendations. We focus on eliminating the current infestation and implementing preventative measures to keep them from returning, ensuring a healthier and more hygienic environment.

Signs You Need Professional Pest Control in Allentown, PA

Recognizing the early signs of a pest infestation is crucial for prompt action and effective control. Ignoring early indicators can lead to more severe and costly problems down the line. Bethlehem Pest Pros encourages Allentown residents and businesses to be vigilant and look for these common signs that indicate it's time to call in the professionals for pest control.

Visible Pest Sightings

This is often the most obvious sign of a pest problem. Seeing live pests, whether it's ants marching across your counter, a mouse scurrying across the floor, or a spider spinning webs in corners, is a clear indication that you have unwanted guests. Even occasional sightings can point to a larger hidden infestation. Don't dismiss a single sighting; it could be the tip of the iceberg. For concerns about specific pests like spiders, visit our spider removal page.

Droppings or Fecal Matter

Pest droppings are a definitive sign of their presence. The size, shape, and location of the droppings can help identify the type of pest. Mouse droppings are small and pellet-shaped, while rat droppings are larger. Cockroach droppings resemble black pepper or coffee grounds. Finding droppings in areas like pantries, cabinets, attics, or basements indicates active pest activity and potential contamination risks. Proper sanitation is essential during and after treatment.

Gnaw Marks or Damaged Property

Rodents, termites, and other pests can cause physical damage to your property through their gnawing and nesting activities. Chewed wires can create fire hazards, damaged insulation can affect energy efficiency, and compromised wood structures can weaken your home's integrity. Look for gnaw marks on baseboards, furniture, electrical wires, or food packaging. Termite damage often appears as hollowed-out wood or mud tubes. Addressing this damage is part of a comprehensive pest management plan.

Unusual Odors

Some pests leave distinct odors. Rodent urine can have a strong, musky smell. Cockroaches may produce an oily or musty odor. The presence of these unusual smells, particularly in enclosed spaces like cabinets or attics, can indicate a pest infestation that requires investigation. Identifying the source of the odor is an important step in locating hidden pests.

Nesting Sites or Materials

Pests build nests for shelter and breeding. Finding nesting materials like shredded paper, fabric, or insulation in secluded areas like attics, wall voids, or crawl spaces is a strong indicator of pest presence. These nesting sites are often hidden and can harbor large populations of pests. Locating and removing nesting sites is crucial for effective pest control.

Scratching or Skittering Noises

Hearing scratching, gnawing, or skittering sounds within walls, ceilings, or floors, particularly at night, often indicates the presence of rodents or other pests moving around your property. These noises can be unsettling and are a clear sign that wildlife or rodents have taken up residence within your home or business. Our wildlife control services address these types of noises specifically.

Bites or Skin Irritations

Unexplained bites or skin irritations can be a sign of pests like bed bugs, fleas, or mosquitos. Bed bug bites often appear in a line or cluster, while flea bites are typically found on legs and ankles. Identifying the source of the bites is essential for targeted treatment and relief. Our bed bug extermination, flea treatment, and mosquito control services can help address these issues.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets may act differently if they detect pests. Dogs might bark or scratch at walls where rodents are present. Cats might intently watch areas where insects are hiding. Pay attention to any unusual behavior from your pets; they might be trying to tell you about a pest problem. For instance, excessive scratching might indicate tick control is needed.

Damaged Plants or Gardens

Some pests, like aphids or certain beetles, primarily affect outdoor plants and gardens. If you notice unexplained damage to your plants, wilting, or visible insects on foliage, it could indicate a garden pest problem that could eventually move closer to your home. Addressing outdoor pests can help prevent them from becoming indoor problems.

Spider Webs in Unusual Locations

While spiders are common, an excessive number of webs in areas that are frequently used or unusual locations can indicate a need for spider removal services. Some dangerous spider species also require professional intervention for safe removal. Our technicians can help you identify problematic spider species and clear your property of their webs and nests.

Presence of Moths in Closets or Pantries

Finding moths fluttering around in your closets or pantry, or discovering damage to clothing or stored food items, suggests a moth infestation. Different types of moths target different materials, such as clothes moths that feed on fabrics and pantry moths that infest dried goods. Targeted treatment is necessary to eliminate the infestation and protect your belongings. Learn about our moth control services.

Silverfish Sightings

Silverfish are nocturnal insects that feed on starches and sugars found in paper, glue, and other common household items. Seeing silverfish in bathrooms, basements, or other damp areas, or finding evidence of their feeding (irregular holes or notches on paper), indicates a silverfish problem. Addressing moisture issues and implementing targeted treatments are key to controlling silverfish. Our silverfish control services can help.

Bee or Wasp Activity Near Your Home

While bees and wasps are beneficial pollinators, nests built close to your home or in high-traffic areas can pose a sting risk, especially for those with allergies. Observing increased activity of bees or wasps, or noticing the formation of a nest, indicates a need for professional bee and wasp removal to ensure safe and effective relocation or extermination. Safety is paramount when dealing with stinging insects.
