Identifying the Need: Common Issues Requiring Raccoon Removal

Recognizing the signs of a raccoon infestation early is crucial for minimizing damage and health risks. Raccoons are nocturnal, so many of the indicators you might notice will occur during the evening or early morning hours. Being vigilant and knowing what to look for can help you determine when it's time to call the professionals for raccoon removal in Bethlehem, PA.

Noises in Attics, Walls, or Chimneys

One of the most common signs of a raccoon presence inside your home is unexplained noise. Raccoons in attics might sound like heavy thumping or scurrying, as they are larger animals than rodents. You might hear scratching, growling, chirping (from young kits), or even walking sounds, especially during the night. These noises can be unsettling and are a strong indicator that wildlife has taken up residence. Ignoring these sounds can lead to significant property damage as the raccoons establish a den and move around.

Damaged Roofs, Soffits, or Vents

Raccoons are strong and resourceful climbers with dexterous paws. They can exploit existing weaknesses in your home's exterior or create new ones to gain entry. Look for torn roofing shingles, bent or damaged soffit panels, or ripped screens on vents. These areas often serve as entry points into attics or wall voids. The damage caused by raccoons trying to gain access or create a den can be extensive and compromise the structural integrity of your home.

Overturned Trash Cans and Scattered Garbage

Known for their scavenging habits, raccoons are notorious for raiding garbage cans in search of food. Finding your trash cans overturned, lids askew, and garbage strewn across your yard is a classic sign you have raccoons visiting your property. While this might seem like a minor annoyance, it indicates that raccoons are actively foraging in your area and may be looking for a sheltered place to den nearby.

Presence of Raccoon Droppings

Raccoon droppings, also known as scat, can be found in various locations around your property, including on decks, patios, sidewalks, or in attics. Raccoon scat is typically tubular and can vary in size and texture depending on their diet. However, it's important to never handle raccoon droppings without proper protection, as they can contain raccoon roundworm eggs, which are harmful to humans. Discovering these droppings is a clear indication of raccoon activity and a potential health hazard that requires professional remediation.

Unpleasant Odors

A persistent, foul odor emanating from your attic, walls, or chimney could be a result of raccoon urine and feces accumulation. These waste products not only create an unpleasant smell but also attract other pests and can cause long-term damage to building materials. The odor can also indicate a deceased animal within the structure, another issue requiring professional assistance for safe removal and sanitation.

Visible Sightings

While primarily nocturnal, raccoons can sometimes be seen during the day, especially if they are foraging for food or if their den has been disturbed. Seeing a raccoon on your property, particularly if it appears disoriented or aggressive, warrants caution and professional intervention. Daytime activity can also indicate a mother raccoon that needs to forage more frequently to support her young.

Damage to Gardens or Landscaping

Raccoons can raid gardens, eating fruits, vegetables, and other plants. They can also dig up lawns in search of grubs or other insects. If you've noticed unexplained damage to your garden or landscaping, raccoons could be the culprits. This type of damage, while not as severe as structural damage, indicates that raccoons are regular visitors to your property in Bethlehem, PA.

Pet Food or Water Dishes Being Raided

Leaving pet food or water outdoors is a major attractant for raccoons. If you find your pet's food dish empty or disturbed overnight, or if you've seen raccoons helping themselves, it's a clear sign they are utilizing your property as a food source. Eliminating easy food access is a critical step in deterring raccoons and is a service we can advise upon and help implement.

Evidence of Nesting Materials

Raccoons build dens using various materials they find, such as leaves, twigs, insulation, or even fabric scraps. Discovering piles of these materials in your attic, chimney, or under a deck suggests raccoons are preparing to or have already established a den. Detecting a den, especially one containing young kits, requires careful handling by professionals to ensure the safety and well-being of the animals during the removal process.
